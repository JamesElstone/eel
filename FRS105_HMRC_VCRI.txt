1. Reference Basis
------------------

Official references checked:

1. FRC FRS 105 page
   https://www.frc.org.uk/library/standards-codes-policy/accounting-and-reporting/uk-accounting-standards/frs-105/
   Relevant baseline: FRS 105 applies to entities using the Micro-entities Regime.

2. FRC FRS 105 September 2024 redacted edition
   https://media.frc.org.uk/documents/FRS_105_September_2024_Redacted_edition_zZFkzvN.pdf
   Relevant baseline: micro-entity financial statements prepared under FRS 105 are presumed to give the true and fair view required by law; a complete set includes a Statement of Financial Position and an Income Statement; recognition and measurement are generally historical cost, with revaluation/fair value routes not permitted. Main 2024 amendments are effective for periods beginning on or after 1 January 2026, with early application permitted if applied together.

3. Companies House micro-entity accounts guidance
   https://www.gov.uk/annual-accounts/microentities-small-and-dormant-companies
   Relevant baseline: micro-entities may prepare/file simpler accounts and must include the required micro-entity balance sheet statements when using those provisions.

4. HMRC Company Tax Return guide
   https://www.gov.uk/guidance/company-tax-return-guide
   Relevant baseline: a Company Tax Return normally consists of the CT600, computations, and accounts. Online filing usually requires accounts and computations in inline XBRL format.

5. HMRC Company Tax Return obligations
   https://www.gov.uk/guidance/company-tax-return-obligations
   Relevant baseline: online filing is required for most company returns; accounts and computations must usually be in inline XBRL; the return is normally due 12 months after the accounting period end; Corporation Tax is normally payable 9 months and 1 day after period end for non-large companies.

6. HMRC Corporation Tax rates and allowances
   https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/rates-and-allowances-corporation-tax/rates-and-allowances-corporation-tax
   Relevant baseline: from 1 April 2023 the main Corporation Tax rate is 25%, with small profits rate and marginal relief rules requiring threshold, associated company, and apportionment handling.

7. HMRC CT600 guide
   https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/corporation-tax-company-tax-return-guide
   Relevant baseline: CT600 boxes, supplementary pages, capital allowances, loss claims, and other disclosures need rule-specific mapping.

8. HMRC Corporation Tax accounting periods
   https://www.gov.uk/corporation-tax-accounting-period
   Relevant baseline: a Corporation Tax accounting period cannot be longer than 12 months.


2. Executive Assessment
-----------------------

The project has a strong conceptual foundation for a micro-entity bookkeeping and tax preparation tool:

- Ledger-first design is the correct direction.
- Double-entry journals are the right source of truth.
- Bank import staging, duplicate controls, source references, and period locks support auditability.
- Trial balance and year-end readiness services provide useful accounting controls.
- The system clearly separates bank transactions, journals, nominal accounts, accounting periods, iXBRL runs, CT600 drafts, and HMRC submission records.
- The HMRC production path is guarded enough that the application should not silently submit an incomplete package.

The current implementation is materially more complete than the original draft-only assessment. It can build a controlled filing basis, generate CT600 V3-shaped XML and accounts/computation iXBRL artifacts, and fail closed when required scope, evidence, integrity, or validation conditions are absent. It remains early-stage software: no successful live HMRC submission or HMRC software recognition is recorded, and no generated artifact should be represented as HMRC-approved or production-recognised.

The highest priority shortfalls are:

1. FRS 105 accounts presentation, comparative information, legal wording, and taxonomy coverage still need continued professional/release review; validation controls do not themselves establish statutory completeness.
2. CT600 V3 XML and CT600A serialization now have local schema-validation coverage, but HMRC end-to-end acceptance and software recognition remain unproven.
3. Computation iXBRL is implemented with a controlled taxonomy/mapping path and validation gates; operational reliance still depends on current artifacts passing both internal and Arelle validation.
4. The Corporation Tax engine remains limited to its documented ordinary-company scope. Augmented profits, ring-fence/special regimes, future-rate maintenance, detailed adjustments, and professional review remain important boundaries.
5. Loss handling uses tax_loss_carryforwards as the canonical source; tax_loss_pools is removed from the baseline schema and dropped by migration for existing installs.
6. Fixed-asset and capital-allowance flows now have materially stronger calculation and fixture coverage, but unusual assets, disposals, and regime-specific cases remain subject to scope gates and review.
7. CT600A/s455 now has an evidence-backed workflow, integrity hashes, section 464A review, section 464C blocking, and separate L2P-claim routing. It is not a substitute for professional review of complex participator-loan cases.
8. The VCRI no longer relies on the historical zero-journal snapshot: the refreshed local database has posted journals and generated calculation/iXBRL runs. That is a readiness indicator, not evidence of a submitted or accepted return.


3. VCRI Control Index
---------------------

VCRI-001 - Correct accounting standard
Reference: FRS 105, micro-entities regime.
Implementation evidence: architecture.md, the accounts/iXBRL page, generated XHTML preview wording, and VCRI file naming now use FRS 105 micro-entity accounts language. The user correction confirms FRS 105 should be the working standard.
Status: OK.
Assessment: Project-facing and user-facing language inspected in this pass is now aligned to FRS 105 micro-entity accounts rather than FRS 102.
Shortfall: None identified in the inspected app text after the wording update. Future pages, generated documents, and tests must preserve this wording.
Remediation: Keep "FRS 105 micro-entity accounts" as the standard phrase and reserve FRS 102 only for entities not applying FRS 105.

VCRI-002 - Accounting period and CT period model
Reference: HMRC CT accounting periods cannot exceed 12 months.
Implementation evidence: ARCHITECTURE.md states that CT periods are derived from confirmed accounting periods. CorporationTaxPeriodService derives and persists sequential statutory CT periods, validates twelve calendar months rather than a fixed 365-day limit, preserves submitted/accepted metadata, and prevents submission of a later CT period until its predecessor is accepted. CtPeriodFilingModelService creates separate immutable CT-period filing bases from a single approved accounting period. test_CtPeriodFilingModelService now exercises a 13-month period from 5 September 2022 to 30 September 2023, deriving 5 September 2022–4 September 2023 and 5–30 September 2023, distinct frozen filing/computation bases, and the filing-order gate. CT600 and computation-iXBRL artifact generation are separately covered by their focused service tests.
Status: OK.
Assessment: The system now automatically derives the statutory split and supports separately frozen, fileable CT-period workflows for the documented ordinary-company scope.
Shortfall: This control does not establish HMRC gateway acceptance, software recognition, or support for tax regimes outside the documented filing scope.
Remediation: Maintain the long-period regression, calendar-boundary coverage, and strict filing-order gate as CT filing functionality evolves.

VCRI-003 - Ledger-first source of truth
Reference: FRS 105 accounts must be supported by reliable books and records; CT computations must reconcile to accounts.
Implementation evidence: architecture.md defines bank plus journals feeding the ledger; TrialBalanceService derives balances from posted journals and journal_lines; journal_lines include checks for one-sided, non-negative debit/credit entries.
Status: OK.
Assessment: Conceptually strong. A posted journal ledger is the right base for accounts and tax.
Shortfall: The observed live database snapshot had zero journals and zero journal_lines, so the current company data was not ready for accounts generation at the time of review.
Remediation: Complete/verify posting workflow for imported transactions before relying on year-end outputs.

VCRI-004 - Double-entry validation
Reference: Accounting records should balance and be traceable.
Implementation evidence: ManualJournalService validates at least two lines, non-zero lines, non-negative amounts, one-sided lines, and total debits equal total credits; TrialBalanceValidationService checks trial balance equality and unposted journals.
Status: OK.
Assessment: The core controls are appropriate and auditable.
Shortfall: More fixture-based tests are needed for edge cases such as reversals, voids, locked periods, transfers, and source rebuilds.
Remediation: Add regression tests around journal balancing, source immutability, and rebuild behaviour.

VCRI-005 - Bank import, staging, and dedupe
Reference: CT/accounting records must support source traceability and avoid duplicate income/expense recognition.
Implementation evidence: statement_uploads, statement_import_rows, transactions.dedupe_hash, workflow status fields, duplicate flags, and source references exist; YearEndMetricsService checks statement continuity and duplicate imports.
Status: OK.
Assessment: The import pipeline has good audit foundations.
Shortfall: It remains dependent on categorisation and posting into journals; imported transactions alone are not accounts.
Remediation: Keep bank import as source data and ensure posting status is visible and blocking for year-end.

VCRI-006 - Nominal accounts and tax treatment model

The Tax Rates page includes a Tax Treatment Rules card with enable/disable controls and AJAX review_status selection.
Status: OK.
Assessment: The app now has a materially suitable reviewable model for CT estimate treatment: nominal tagging remains the fallback, while sourced treatment rules handle known disallowables, capital items, ordinary revenue fallbacks, and review-required categories.
Shortfall: Seeded and needs_review rules still require accountant/tax adviser review before filing reliance. The current catalogue is an initial rule set, not a comprehensive CT legislation engine.
Remediation: Continue adding sourced rules as new nominal categories are introduced, promote reviewed rules to review_status=reviewed after professional review, and add fixture tests for known disallowable and review-required categories.

VCRI-008 - FRS 105 financial statements

VCRI-012 - Corporation Tax computation rate engine
Reference: HMRC rates from 1 April 2023 include 25% main rate, 19% small profits rate, thresholds, marginal relief, associated companies, and period apportionment.
Implementation evidence: corporation_tax_rate_rules stores versioned financial-year rates and HMRC source metadata. CorporationTaxRateService loads active rules from that table and calculates pre-April 2023 19% flat rate plus post-April 2023 main rate, small profits rate, marginal relief, associated company threshold reduction, and financial-year apportionment; CorporationTaxComputationService calls that service. The Tax Rates card now renders the rate rules through TableFramework with an Active/All filter defaulting to Active, five-row pagination, filtered export support, and a retained HMRC refresh action.
Status: PARTIAL.
Assessment: The flat-rate defect has been remediated for ordinary non-ring-fence micro-company estimates, and active versus superseded rate rules are now easier to review in the UI.
Shortfall: The calculation still assumes non-ring-fence profits and, unless extended, augmented profits equal taxable profits. The new table improves reviewability but does not replace annual rate maintenance, professional review, unusual CT regime handling, or final CT600 box validation.
Remediation: Add explicit augmented profit inputs, accountant review flags, annual rate-rule migration review, future-rate update tests, and final CT600 traceability from each rate-band calculation.

4. Material Shortfall Register
------------------------------

S-001 - Accounts iXBRL production assurance remains incomplete
Severity: Medium/High.
Issue: A controlled accounts iXBRL generation and validation path exists, but statutory completeness, external acceptance, release evidence, and software recognition are not yet established.
Impact: An artifact may be technically validated without being safe to represent as HMRC-approved or a complete statutory accounts pack.
Fix: Maintain taxonomy and artifact validation, complete presentation/release review, and collect documented sandbox/live-recognition evidence.

S-002 - CT600 gateway acceptance and recognition remain unproven
Severity: Medium/High.
Issue: CT600 V3 generation and local XSD validation exist for the supported path, including CT600A, but no live HMRC acceptance or recognition is recorded.
Impact: Local schema validity is necessary but not enough to establish production filing readiness.
Fix: Preserve box-level traceability, run controlled sandbox/TIL submissions, and retain evidence before enabling live filing.

S-003 - Computation iXBRL requires current external validation
Severity: Medium/High.
Issue: Computation iXBRL generation exists but is deliberately unavailable without a frozen filing basis, approved mapping, current taxonomy resources, and successful structural/Arelle validation.
Impact: A missing, stale, changed, or failed artifact blocks submission.
Fix: Maintain package provenance and external validation evidence; collect sandbox/TIL acceptance before production use.

S-004 - Corporation Tax rate engine still needs production hardening
Severity: Medium/High.
Issue: The flat 19% rate has been replaced with a database-backed rate engine, but assumptions remain around non-ring-fence profits and augmented profits.
Impact: Ordinary estimates are materially improved, and the Tax Rates table now exposes active versus superseded rules for review, but unusual CT cases can still be wrong.

7. Overall Conclusion
---------------------

The project is moving in the right architectural direction. Its strongest controls are now the ledger-first model, double-entry validation, import staging/dedupe, deterministic accounting/tax checks, year-end and filing-basis locks, validation-gated iXBRL artifacts, CT600 V3/CT600A generation, and auditable scope/evidence controls.

The main remaining gap is external production assurance, not the absence of a filing implementation. Local generation, schema checks, controlled artifacts, and focused tests are meaningful progress, but they do not establish HMRC gateway acceptance, formal software recognition, or a successful live submission.

Recommended current positioning:

- Suitable for controlled bookkeeping, categorisation, review, calculation, filing-artifact preparation, and evidence-gated test/sandbox workflows within the documented supported scope.
- Suitable for identifying missing source data, uncategorised transactions, suspense, unposted journals, ledger readiness issues, unresolved scope questions, and stale/failed validation artifacts.
- Not yet to be represented as HMRC-approved, production-recognised, or proven by a successful live HMRC submission. Final statutory, tax, and filing decisions still require appropriate professional review and the remaining external assurance steps.
